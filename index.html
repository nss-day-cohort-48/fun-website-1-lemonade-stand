There’s a lot that goes into making a webpage work. If you start by learning the building blocks of laying out your page (HTML or Hyper Text Markup Language) and styling it to look great on any device (CSS or Cascading Style Sheets), you’ll have a lot of flexibility in how your page looks.

Add in using Command Line on your terminal to speed up the work and you’ll have powerful tools at your disposal.

HTML is the language you use to add your content. Think of it as the framework that holds all the text, images and other media you add to your page. That’s just the start, though. It can do a whole lot more! Read more.

CSS lets you choose what colors and fonts you want. It also makes your webpage responsive, so your site works as well on your phone as on your laptop or table. CSSRead more.

Command Line is the fast lane for writing your code. Developers save themselves hours by weaning themselves off the mouse and the trackpad and over to keyboard commands. Commit to learning command line and working in your terminal – it’s a critical step in becoming a developer. Read more.
